About Kullu

Kullu, often referred to as the 'Valley of Gods', is a beautiful hill station located in the Kullu Valley, Himachal Pradesh. Known for its stunning landscapes, vibrant culture, and religious significance, it is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the region. Surrounded by snow-capped mountains, lush greenery, and the Beas River, Kullu offers a serene and tranquil atmosphere.

Culture

Kullu’s culture is deeply rooted in local traditions, with a significant influence of Hinduism and Tibetan Buddhism. The town is famous for its colorful Kullu Dussehra festival, a week-long celebration that attracts visitors from all over India. The valley is also known for its distinctive music, dance, and handlooms.

Food

Kullu's cuisine features traditional Himachali dishes such as Siddu (steamed dough stuffed with spices), Chana Madra (chickpeas in a spicy gravy), and Patande (sweet pancakes). The food is simple yet flavorful, often made using fresh local ingredients.
